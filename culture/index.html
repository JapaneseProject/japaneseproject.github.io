Education

To the Japanese, the love of learning is an important virtue of a man's life.

Eduation is a big part of Japanese culture.

Education isn't focused on critical thinking, it's more focused on test taking.

If parents find the schooling unsatisfactory, they can get a private tutor or send their kids to private schools.

Formal education didn't come to Japan until the Chinese language system, which was adopted in 500 CE. Before then, only the rich could afford education.

Values (Besides Education)

They keep their feelings to themselves, as is it perceived as strange if you display your emotions in public.

This is because of Tatemae, or the belief that there is a divide between one's public expression and their private opinions.

They learn that their life is interdependant with others and they are taught a set of beliefs where fulfillment is gained through close association with others.

Social Customs

People bow to their superiors to show respect.

They will usually say 'Itadakimasu' ('let's eat') before eating.

They never tip their waiters and waitresses because that would be a rating of their work.

They never draw attention to themselves and will usually just focus on their own social group.

Food

Common Japanese foods include sushia and sashimi, udon, and miso soup.

The food is seasonal and is based on the importance of being grateful for what you have and taking advantage of it.

Architecture

The architecture in Japan is usually made from wood and elevated from the ground. Inside, sliding doors are used instead of normal doors so that the interior is comfortable and can be used for multiple occasions.

Land Use

Japan has twelve different types of zones for commercial, industrial, and residential uses. Japan also have regulations for types of facilities that can be built in certain areas. Agriculture is also big in Japan. Many people are farmers and work at home. In most rural areas, rice farms are ubiquitous.

Religion

The main religion of Japan is Shintoism where people believe there is sort of an after life as a spirit and where your ancestors spirits guard over you. Many people are also Buddhist. Shinto has existed for as long as the Japanese culture, while Buddhism came later. The two religions have coexisted for a long time.

Language

Surprisingly, the main language of Japan is Japanese. There are 3 main types that Japanese people use to write. They are Hiragana, Katakana, and Kanji. Hiragana is the most simple, while Kanji is similar to chinese characters and is much more complicated and difficult.
